# Learning Path

Build With Agents can be used in different ways depending on your background.

The official course is here: [buildwithagents.vercel.app](https://buildwithagents.vercel.app/).

## If you are new to Claude Code

Recommended pace: one day per calendar day.

Focus on:

- Reading each workflow before running it.
- Asking Claude Code to explain its plan before execution.
- Keeping notes on what broke.
- Avoiding shortcuts on review gates.

Do not rush Day 1. The WAT model is the base for the rest of the course.

## If you are an operator

Recommended pace: two to four days in the first week, then apply one build to a real process.

Focus on:

- Replacing one recurring task.
- Keeping outputs visible and inspectable.
- Making approval steps explicit.
- Turning repeated work into skills.

Best days for immediate ROI:

- Day 1 for repeatable writing and research workflows.
- Day 3 for reusable skills.
- Day 6 for scheduled routines.
- Day 7 for personal operating context.

## If you are an analyst

Recommended pace: complete Days 1 to 3 first, then spend extra time on Days 4 and 6.

Focus on:

- Data collection workflows.
- Structured outputs.
- Tool choice and fallback rules.
- Logs, run notes, and reproducibility.

Best days for analyst workflows:

- Day 2 for scraping and MCP.
- Day 4 for deployment.
- Day 6 for scheduled reporting.

## If you are a founder or PM

Recommended pace: complete the full week, then adapt the capstone to a real product or GTM process.

Focus on:

- Fast prototypes that produce real artifacts.
- Guardrails for sending, publishing, and deleting.
- Clear demos your team can inspect.
- Turning useful workflows into repeatable team processes.

Best days for product thinking:

- Day 1 for workflow design.
- Day 5 for frontend iteration.
- Day 7 for context architecture.

## If you are a developer

Recommended pace: move quickly through Days 1 to 3, then slow down on deployment and reliability.

Focus on:

- Separating agent instructions from deterministic code.
- Adding observability.
- Avoiding hidden state.
- Writing docs that future agents can read.

Best days for engineering habits:

- Day 4 for deployment boundaries.
- Day 6 for scheduled behavior.
- Day 7 for maintainable context.

## Suggested completion rhythm

| Time available | Recommended path |
| --- | --- |
| 30 minutes | Read the homepage, star the repo, skim this learning path |
| 2 hours | Complete Day 1 only |
| Weekend | Complete Days 1 to 3 |
| 1 week | Complete all 7 days |
| 2 weeks | Complete the full course plus capstone |

## What to keep after the course

Keep:

- Your best workflow.
- Your first useful skill.
- One deployed automation.
- Your EA folder.
- Your decision log.
- A list of the next three automations worth building.

Delete or archive:

- Experiments that do not replace real work.
- Prompts that only worked once.
- Automations with no logs.
- Anything that sends or publishes without approval rules.
